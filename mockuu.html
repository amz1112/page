Malaria Assessment, Jan - April,2019

Malaria Risk Assessment in India I have been working on assessment of malarial risk in India from 2009-2018. The project focuses on isolating the socio-economic and bio-physical parameters that contribute to Malarial risk in India. A trend Analysis, both paramteric and non-parametric was carried out. Dependent variable for regression was smoothed incidence of Malaria. Independent variables include primarily Environemntal variables like precipitation, mean temperature, presence of water sources etc. Socio economic parameters include literacy(both rural and urban), average income, presence of scheduled tribe population. The variables were decided after extensive review of similar works and research in this regard. The softwares used are Google Earth Engine, Python and R.

Statistical Analysis: Cluster-Outlier Analysis was performed to understand autocorrelation. Incase of existence of autocorrelation efficiency of Lag and Error Models were checked. Throughout the 10 years, most years showed significant presence of spatial auto-correlation. GeoPy was used to carry out spatial regression.

Spatio Temporal Analaysis using Google Earth Engine

Spatio-temporal analysis of Wetlands - A Case Study of Kuttanad, Kerala using Google Earth Engine May-july, 2018

The Analysis Why Kuttanad? Kuttanad is known as the rice bowl of Kerala. Its one of the most beautiful places in the God's own Country. However, over the years Kuttanad has faced recurring floods, surface water loss, increased salinity of soil and water run off which has had enormous impact on the lives of the local people. My study focused on quantifying this impact and identifying the potential drivers that caused it. The entire study was carried out using Google Earth Engine. All data was calculated and extracted from satellite images. No field work was done. Flow accumulations were calculated from the images to understand why the region floods occasionally. Flooded regions were mapped from Sentinel images by acquiring images from a before and an after date. Image smoothing techniques like canny edge detector and hough transform were used. Surface water loss was quantified by using GSW layers and creating a dictionary that associated transition class values with their names, and colour coding it with a custom palette. The result was then mapped.

Time Series Analysis A time series analysisof NDVI and NDWI was carried out by extracting values from Landsat Images for 14 years. Parametric trend analysis was then done.

URBAN in the Indian Context

A study which attempted to quantify the term 'URBAN' in the Indian context.August-Decmber,2019

The Analysis According to the census of 2011,only 31% of India is urban, but satellite images had a different story to tell.

How are the Indian Urban policies decided? Inorder to understand the real Urban context, population variables were chosen from Census of 2011 and 2001 and compared to the data collected from GHSL population and urbanisation grids. The proximity to transporation corridors were also examined for five major Indian cities. It was understood that many well developed Municipal towns and panchayats can be reclassified and given a town or city status and maybe the urban criteria can be modified so that these smaller regions can benefit from the urban funds. The study was carried out using Google Earth Engine, R, Python, ArcPy and ArcEnterprise.

I used random forest classification to classify all five cities to confirm presence of builtup in the images and compared it to the urban grids provided by GHSL. The results were very comparable proving the effectiveness of GHSL for urban studies. Further on raster results were converted to vectors and these patches were individually analysed in case of all 5 major cities. Correlation analysis was carried out in R.
